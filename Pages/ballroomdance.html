Ballroom Dance

History

The culture of ballroom dance first came in 18 and 19 centuries
in the western Europe where aristocrats danced ro express
their emotions and vibes. Since then, different civilizations cultivated
different forms of ballroom dance. It is an indication of
high standards, romance and tenderness cherished by people of high standards.
Five kind of ballroom dances feature in international ballroom dance competitions.

Viennese Waltz

The austrian-style ballroom waltz named
by its capital Vienna, has faster tempo than 'English' waltz.
Still, it is widely danced as an social enjoyment.
In some places, it is known as 'Waltz' and
standard waltz is known 'English' waltz.

Waltz

Also known as 'English' waltz, the 18 century
austrian folk dance, has the ability to
orchestrate the inner-love. Soft tender
body movements make it elegant and suitable
to celebrate and mark special occasions.

Tango

A powerful energetic dance, embedded
with South American essence.
This swifty dance is one of the five ballroom dances
recognized all over the world.

Foxtrot

This slow-tempoed dance was originated
from Tin Pan Ally in the 1920s.
Slow mesmerizing movements make up
the trademark of this dance.

Quickstep

WIth firery kicks and steps, Quickstep brings
foxtrot dance to a faster and livelier experience.
Charleston was the first pioneer of this energetic dance.
